/* AN ACT TO PROVIDE APPROPRIATIONS FOR THE FISCAL YEAR ENDING SEPTEMBER 30, 2025, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES, TO MAKE FURTHER RELATED TO TAX POLICY, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES. 112002.DEFINITION OF QUALIFYING ENTITY FOR ENERGY CREDITS (a)Definitions (1)Section 7701(a) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraphs: (48)Foreign-controlled entityFor purposes of this section, the term foreign-controlled entity means— (A)the government of a covered nation (as defined in section 4872(f)(2) of title 10, United States Code), (B)a person who is a citizen, national, or resident of a covered nation, provided that such person is not an individual who is a citizen or lawful permanent resident of the United States, (C)an entity or a qualified business unit (as defined in section 989(a)) incorporated or organized under the laws of, or having its principal place of business in, a covered nation, or (D)an entity (including subsidiary entities) controlled (as determined under subparagraph (F)) by an entity described in subparagraph (A), (B), or (C). (49)Foreign-influenced entityFor purposes of this section, the term foreign-influenced entity means an entity— (A)with respect to which, during the taxable year— (i)a specified foreign entity has the direct or indirect authority to appoint a covered officer of such entity, (ii)a single specified foreign entity owns at least 10 percent of such entity, (iii)one or more specified foreign entities own in the aggregate at least 25 percent of such entity, or (iv)at least 25 percent of the debt of such entity is held in the aggregate by one or more specified foreign entities, or (B)which, during the previous taxable year— (i)makes a payment of dividends, interest, compensation for services, rentals or royalties, guarantees or any other fixed, determinable, annual, or periodic amount to a specified foreign entity in an amount which is equal to or greater than 10 percent of the total of such payments made by such entity during such taxable year, or (ii)makes payments described in subclause (I) to more than 1 specified foreign entity in an amount which, in the aggregate, is equal to or greater than 25 percent of the total of such payments made by such entity during such taxable year. Clause (ii) shall not apply unless such entity makes such payments knowingly (or has reason to know). (50)Covered officerFor purposes of this section, the term covered officer means, with respect to an entity— (A)a member of the board of directors, board of supervisors, or equivalent governing body, (B)an executive-level officer, including the president, chief executive officer, chief operating officer, chief financial officer, general counsel, or senior vice president, or (C)an individual having powers or responsibilities similar to those of officers or members described in subparagraph (A) or (B). (51)Determination of controlFor purposes of subparagraph (48)(D), the term control means— (A)in the case of a corporation, ownership (by vote or value) of more than 50 percent of the stock in such corporation, (B)in the case of a partnership, ownership of more than 50 percent of the profits interests or capital interests in such partnership, or (C)in any other case, ownership of more than 50 percent of the beneficial interests in the entity. (52)Determination of ownershipFor purposes of this section, section 318 (other than subsection (a)(3) thereof) shall apply for purposes of determining ownership of stock in a corporation. Similar principles shall apply for purposes of determining ownership of interests in any other entity. (53)Regulations and guidanceThe Secretary may prescribe such regulations and guidance as may be necessary or appropriate to carry out the provisions of this paragraph. (54)Material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity (A)In generalThe term material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity means, with respect to any property— (i)any component, subcomponent, or applicable critical mineral (as defined in section 45X(c)(6)) included in such property that is extracted, processed, recycled, manufactured, or assembled by a prohibited foreign entity, or (ii)any design of such property which is based on any copyright or patent held by a prohibited foreign entity or any know-how or trade secret provided by a prohibited foreign entity. (B)Exclusion (i)In generalThe term material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity shall not include any assembly part or constituent material, provided that such part or material is not acquired directly from a prohibited foreign entity. (ii)Assembly partFor purposes of this subparagraph, the term assembly part means a subcomponent or collection of subcomponents which is— (I)not uniquely designed for use in the construction of a qualified facility described in section 45Y or 48E or an eligible component described in section 45X, and (II)not exclusively or predominantly produced by prohibited foreign entities. (iii)Constituent materialFor purposes of this subparagraph, the term constituent material means any material which is— (I)not uniquely formulated for use in a qualified facility described in section 45Y or 48E or an eligible component described in section 45X, and (II)not exclusively or predominantly produced, processed, or extracted by prohibited foreign entities. (iv)Regulations and guidanceThe Secretary may prescribe such regulations and guidance as may be necessary or appropriate to carry out the provisions of this paragraph. 112003.DENIAL OF CREDIT FOR EXPENDITURES FOR CERTAIN WIND AND SOLAR LEASING ARRANGEMENTS (d)Denial of credit for expenditures for wind and solar leasing arrangementsSection 45Y, as amended by subsection (a), is amended by inserting after subsection (c) the following new subsection: (d)Denial of credit for expenditures for wind and solar leasing arrangementsNo credit shall be allowed under this section for any investment during the taxable year with respect to property described in paragraph (1), (2), or (4) of section 25D(d) if— (1)the taxpayer rents or leases such property to a third party during such taxable year, and (2)the lessee would qualify for a credit under section 25D with respect to such property if the lessee owned such property. 112004.EFFECTIVE DATES (e)Effective dates (1)In generalExcept as provided in paragraph (2), the amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after the date of enactment of this Act. (112001-112018)** This bill summary outlines several amendments to the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, primarily focusing on tax policy related to energy credits, foreign entities, sports franchises, and state and local taxes (SALT). **Areas Affected:** 1. **Energy Credits (Sections 112002, 112003, 112005, 112014):** * **Definition of Qualifying Entity:** Introduces new definitions for "foreign-controlled entity," "foreign-influenced entity," "covered officer," "control," and "ownership" specifically for energy credit purposes. * **Material Assistance:** Defines "material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity" related to components, subcomponents, critical minerals, designs, and know-how used in property. * **Denial of Credit for Leasing:** Denies credits for wind and solar property (Section 45Y) if the property is leased to a third party and the lessee would qualify for the credit if they owned it. * **Restrictions on Clean Electricity Investment Credit (Section 48E):** * Terminates the credit for facilities beginning construction after specific dates or placed in service after December 31, 2028 (depending on facility type). * Denies credits for facilities or energy storage technology where construction begins after December 31, 2025, if they include "material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity." * Denies credits to taxpayers who are "specified foreign entities" or "foreign-influenced entities" after certain dates. * Denies credits to taxpayers making significant payments (dividends, interest, royalties, etc.) to prohibited foreign entities related to electricity production or storage. * Introduces a "recapture" mechanism for credits previously allowed if "applicable payments" (e.g., dividends, interest) are made to prohibited foreign entities within 10 years of placing property in service. * **Phase-out and Restrictions on Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (Section 45X):** * Phases out the credit percentage for certain components starting in calendar year 2032. * Terminates the credit for wind energy components sold after December 31, 2027. * Denies credits for eligible components produced after December 31, 2025, if they include "material assistance from a prohibited foreign entity" or are produced under licensing agreements with such entities. * Denies credits to taxpayers who are "specified foreign entities" or "foreign-influenced entities" after certain dates. * Denies credits to taxpayers making significant payments (dividends, interest, royalties, etc.) to prohibited foreign entities related to the production of eligible components. * **Repeal of Transferability:** Repeals the ability to transfer certain credits, including the Clean Fuel Production Credit (Section 112010), Carbon Oxide Sequestration Credit (Section 112011), Zero-Emission Nuclear Power Production Credit (Section 112012), and Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit (Section 112014). * **Phase-out of Energy Property Credit (Section 112015):** * Phases out the energy percentage for certain energy property (Section 48(a)(3)(vii)) based on construction start dates. * Denies credits for such property to taxpayers who are "specified foreign entities" or "foreign-influenced entities" after certain dates. * **Termination of Nuclear Power Credit (Section 112012):** Accelerates the termination date of the credit from December 31, 2032, to December 31, 2031. * **Termination of Clean Hydrogen Credit (Section 112013):** Accelerates the termination date of the credit from January 1, 2033, to January 1, 2026. 2. The dollar amounts are temporarily increased for 2027-2033. * **Partnerships and S Corporations:** Amends Section 702(a)(6) to include "specified taxes" (Section 275(b)) and "substitute payments" (Section 275(b)(5)) as items taken into account by partners/shareholders. Amends Section 703(a)(2)(B) to disallow deductions for these taxes/payments at the partnership level. * **Tax Allocation Mismatch:** Introduces a new section (6659) to impose an additional tax on "covered individuals" who receive "specified tax benefits" from partnerships related to certain "partnership specified tax payments" (under Section 275(b)). Defines how to calculate the "dollar value" of specified tax benefits. * **Capitalization of Specified Taxes:** Amends Section 275(c) to prevent "specified taxes" (Section 275(b)) from being charged to capital account by individuals. * **Reporting:** Amends Sections 6031 (partnerships) and 6037 (S corporations) to require reporting on whether they have gross receipts from "specified service trade or businesses" (Section 199A(d)(2)). * **Temporary Increase for 2025:** Temporarily increases the SALT deduction limit (Section 164(b)(6)) to $20,000 ($10,000 for married filing separately) for the taxable year 2025.
